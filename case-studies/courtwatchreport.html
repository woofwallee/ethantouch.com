Challenge

Twenty-eight pages of legal analysis: protective order outcomes, judge-by-judge variation, dismissal patterns, petitioner support, methodology, and policy recommendations. Dense, important, and completely undesigned. It needed to hold up under scrutiny and still be something a normal person could actually sit down and read.

The writing was already done. Nobody needed me to touch the analysis. What they needed was someone to take all of that content and make it look like the serious report it actually was.

That meant charts, section breaks, callout boxes, photography, and a layout system that could carry heavy content without burying the reader in it.

Approach

Designed the full report from scratch — every page built around its content, not dropped into a template.

Created a unified system: cover, section headers, recurring layouts, chart pages, callout boxes, photography placement, and recommendation pages.

Broke the heaviest sections (methodology, findings, discussion, recommendations) into scannable layouts with charts and callouts surfacing the key patterns.

Prepped for publishing and distribution — launch-ready the day it was done.

Results
Produced a fully designed 28-page year-end report
Turned dense research and legal analysis into a more readable digital document
Built a consistent visual system across cover, section breaks, charts, findings, discussion, and recommendations
Made key outcomes easier to scan through stronger hierarchy, charts, and callout design
Delivered a finished report ready to post and share the day it was done
Gave the organization a flagship report strong enough to hand to funders, partners, and the public
