Challenge

The page had a solid foundation. It explained the program, captured leads, and gave students a clear next step. But it was leaving conversions on the table.

Tracking was incomplete. One email capture path had a broken integration. The sign-up flow had an extra confirmation step adding unnecessary friction. On mobile, the layout broke in places and copy got hard to read. The form had fields that made the data messy on the back end.

The page didn't need a redesign. It needed someone to go through it carefully, find the gaps, and tell the team exactly what to fix.

Approach

Audited the page across five areas: tracking, lead capture, form experience, messaging, and mobile performance.

Found a missing conversion signal on one sign-up path and a broken integration killing email capture. Highest-priority fixes.

Flagged an extra confirmation step adding friction, plus form fields that needed simplification for cleaner data capture.

Mobile had real problems — broken spacing, poor readability, layout collapse. Desktop was fine. Mobile was not.

Tightened copy, removed clutter, moved the program's value proposition higher on the page.

Recommended audience-specific landing page variants instead of routing all traffic to one generic page.
