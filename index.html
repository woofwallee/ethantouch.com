Challenge

Upstream USA provides free contraceptive care services, training, and resources to hospitals and clinics across the country. The organization generated leads from multiple channels — 5–10 healthcare conferences per year (each producing 100 to 1,000+ contacts), quarterly webinars, paid media campaigns, form submissions, and white paper downloads. Across all channels, the team was processing thousands of leads annually.

But there was no system to sort them. When a lead list came in, a team member had to manually vet hundreds, sometimes thousands of contacts on a spreadsheet — cross-referencing whether they were new leads, existing partners, or disqualified contacts. The process took hours to days, creating friction and delays in outreach. By the time leads heard from Upstream, the momentum from the initial interaction had faded, and conversion numbers suffered.

HubSpot had just been launched, but it wasn't yet connected to the Salesforce instance that the 10+ person recruitment team relied on daily.

Approach

Built five dedicated lead routing workflows in HubSpot — one for each inbound channel: conferences, webinars, paid media, form submissions, and content downloads. Each workflow followed the same core logic but tagged and sorted leads based on how they first engaged with Upstream, then routed them into channel-specific drip campaigns with messaging tailored to that interaction. Created 60+ custom HubSpot properties mapped to Salesforce fields to ensure accurate data sync across both platforms.

Each workflow automatically classified contacts as MQL or SQL based on defined criteria, assigned lifecycle stages, tagged the source event or campaign, and scored leads accordingly. Custom properties auto-populated the source of each contact's interaction, adding personalization to outreach and reminding leads how they first connected with Upstream.

When a new lead came in from a company that already had an existing relationship with Upstream, the workflow detected the match and updated the contact record accordingly — tagging them as an existing prospect or partner rather than a new lead. This prevented overlapping communications and ensured no one received messaging that contradicted what their colleague had already been told.

All MQL and SQL activity synced automatically to Salesforce, where the recruitment team managed their pipeline. SQLs entered automated drip campaigns — 3 to 6 emails per series — introducing Upstream's services and resources with a Calendly CTA for scheduling a meeting. MQLs were routed to Salesforce as a separate lead set for manual discovery by the recruitment team. Appointment bookings through Calendly triggered internal notifications and logged all details to the contact record — replacing the old process of manually forwarding emails asking for follow-ups.

Designed a standardized email template system — branding, graphics, and layout — using Beefree for email design, then deployed through HubSpot. Managed the full email lifecycle: subject line strategy, list targeting, suppression lists, A/B testing on send times and subject lines, and post-send reporting. Each department's drip campaign was built, then handed off to a trained point person to monitor, with escalation back for updates, fixes, or improvements.

Partnered directly with the team member responsible for collecting lead lists to define exactly what data was required before import. Built a shared email workflow so all stakeholders had visibility into incoming lists, and established a clear handoff: the list was formatted, sent to a shared inbox for review, then forwarded for HubSpot import — eliminating the confusion of who had the list and who to contact when issues came up.

Built a custom spreadsheet with formulas that calculated open rates, engagement, bounce rates, readability, unsubscribes, and list size against historical data — because the HubSpot subscription tier didn't support the reporting depth the team needed. This became the go-to resource for team meetings and end-of-year reporting. An annual email performance report generated attention from senior leadership, including the CEO.

Results
99% reduction in manual lead vetting — hours/days to near-instant sorting
24-hour lead response — replacing significant delays in first outreach
50+ workflows built across lead routing, drip campaigns, data hygiene, and operations
Full pipeline visibility across MQL/SQL stages for all teams
Automated scheduling replaced manual email forwarding via Calendly integration
Improved open rates through timely, channel-specific messaging
Better segmentation — automated tagging by source, lifecycle, and company
Cross-team alignment — all activity visible across marketing, recruitment, and ops

Core Workflows
Challenge

Millennium Forum — a nonprofit dedicated to educator well-being — relied on a fragmented collection of disconnected tools to manage its membership program. No central database existed. Every time a new member enrolled, staff had to manually track registration, assign meeting groups, send onboarding emails, and coordinate across spreadsheets and disconnected platforms.

The lack of integration between platforms resulted in significant manual work at every stage — from signup to scheduling to member communication. The system couldn't scale, and with ambitious membership growth goals, the manual approach was becoming unsustainable.

Approach

Started with a full tech stack audit — interviewing staff, mapping existing workflows, and evaluating every platform in the ecosystem. Identified HubSpot as the right foundation: a central CRM that could unify member data and power automation across the organization.

Connected nine platforms into a single integrated stack: Squarespace (website), Hivebrite (member community), Circles (online meetings), Zoom (webinars), Google Sheets (existing data), Gmail (staff communication), Shopify and ReCharge Payments (eCommerce/subscriptions), and SurveyMonkey (feedback) — all linked through native integrations and Zapier.

Migrated all existing member data from legacy systems and spreadsheets into HubSpot, restructuring the contact model with custom properties to capture the unique information the program required.

Discovered a vulnerability in the existing eCommerce setup and led the migration to Shopify with ReCharge Payments — building a subscription-based membership system that synced back to the CRM through proprietary plugins and Zapier.

Built three core automated workflows in HubSpot: a registration and welcome series for new members, automated meeting reminders with personalized time slots, group numbers, and meeting links, and lifecycle communications that guided members through the full program experience.

Designed the email system end-to-end — templates, branding, automation sequences — so members received timely, relevant communications at every stage without staff intervention.

Conducted hands-on training sessions for the Millennium Forum team and created detailed documentation so staff could manage the system independently. Provided ongoing MarTech support for troubleshooting and optimization.

Built a custom reporting spreadsheet to track open rates, engagement, bounces, readability, and list performance against historical data — filling a gap the HubSpot subscription tier couldn't cover. This became the team's go-to resource for meetings and annual reporting.

Results
400+ members enrolled and processed through automated workflows
65% email open rate across member communications
9 platforms integrated into a unified stack with HubSpot as the hub
Full automation of registration, onboarding, and member communications
Subscription commerce built from scratch on Shopify + ReCharge
Staff independence — team trained and managing day-to-day operations
Custom reporting that surfaced insights the CRM tier couldn't provide
Scalable foundation — system designed to grow with membership goals
