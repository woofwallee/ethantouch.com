Lead Response Time
Challenge

Upstream USA works with hospitals and clinics on contraceptive care. They pulled leads from 5 to 10 healthcare conferences a year, sometimes 1,000+ contacts per event. Add webinars, paid campaigns, form fills, white paper downloads. Thousands of leads annually, coming in from everywhere.

No system to sort any of it. Someone sat with a spreadsheet and manually cross-referenced every contact: new lead, existing partner, or disqualified. That took hours. Sometimes days. By the time anyone followed up, the lead had gone cold.

HubSpot was live but not connected to Salesforce, which the 10+ person recruitment team used every day.

Approach

I built five lead routing workflows in HubSpot, one for each inbound channel: conferences, webinars, paid media, form submissions, and content downloads. Same core logic across all five, but each one tagged and sorted leads based on how they first interacted with Upstream, then routed them into channel-specific drip campaigns. I also created 60+ custom properties mapped to Salesforce fields so data stayed accurate across both platforms.

Each workflow classified contacts as MQL or SQL automatically, assigned lifecycle stages, tagged the source event or campaign, and scored leads. Custom properties captured how the contact first connected with Upstream, which made outreach more personal and gave the team better context before every conversation.

If a new lead came in from a company that already had a relationship with Upstream, the workflow caught it. It updated the record, tagged them as an existing prospect or partner, and prevented overlapping communications. Nobody got messaging that contradicted what their colleague had already heard.

MQL and SQL activity synced to Salesforce automatically. SQLs went into drip campaigns, 3 to 6 emails per series, with a Calendly link for scheduling. MQLs were routed to Salesforce as a separate set for the recruitment team to work manually. Calendly bookings triggered internal notifications and logged everything to the contact record, replacing the old process of forwarding emails around asking someone to follow up.

I designed a standardized email template system in Beefree (branding, graphics, layout) and deployed it through HubSpot. I handled the full email process: subject lines, list targeting, suppression, A/B testing on timing and copy, and post-send reporting. Each department's drip campaign was built and then handed off to a trained point person, with a path back to me for updates or fixes.

I worked directly with the person collecting lead lists to define exactly what data was needed before import. We set up a shared email workflow so everyone could see incoming lists, and created a clear handoff: format the list, send to a shared inbox for review, then forward for HubSpot import. That cut out the confusion of who had the list and who to bug about it.

HubSpot's subscription tier didn't support the reporting depth the team needed, so I built a custom spreadsheet that tracked open rates, engagement, bounces, readability, unsubscribes, and list size against historical data. It became the go-to resource for team meetings and year-end reporting. The annual email performance report got attention from senior leadership, including the CEO.

Results
99% reduction in manual lead vetting, from hours/days to near-instant sorting
24-hour lead response where there used to be significant delays
50+ workflows built across lead routing, drip campaigns, data hygiene, and operations
Full pipeline visibility across MQL/SQL stages for all teams
Automated scheduling replaced manual email forwarding via Calendly integration
Improved open rates through timely, channel-specific messaging
Better segmentation through automated tagging by source, lifecycle, and company
Cross-team alignment with all activity visible across marketing, recruitment, and ops

Core Workflows
Challenge

Millennium Forum helps educators with well-being and professional development. They managed memberships across disconnected tools with no central database. Every new enrollment meant manually tracking registration, assigning groups, sending onboarding emails, and bouncing between spreadsheets.

Nothing talked to anything else. Signup, scheduling, member communication, all manual. The team had growth goals but no way to operate at that scale.

Approach

I started with a full tech stack audit. Interviewed staff, mapped existing workflows, and looked at every platform they were using. HubSpot made sense as the foundation: one central CRM to unify member data and run automation across the org.

I connected nine platforms into one stack: Squarespace (website), Hivebrite (member community), Circles (online meetings), Zoom (webinars), Google Sheets (existing data), Gmail (staff communication), Shopify and ReCharge Payments (eCommerce/subscriptions), and SurveyMonkey (feedback). Everything linked through native integrations and Zapier.

I migrated all existing member data from legacy systems and spreadsheets into HubSpot, then restructured the contact model with custom properties to capture the specific information the program needed.

I found a vulnerability in the existing eCommerce setup and led the migration to Shopify with ReCharge Payments. Built a subscription-based membership system that synced back to the CRM through proprietary plugins and Zapier.

Three core workflows in HubSpot: a registration and welcome series for new members, automated meeting reminders with personalized time slots and group numbers, and lifecycle communications that walked members through the full program.

I designed the full email system: templates, branding, automation sequences. Members got the right communication at the right time without anyone on staff having to send it manually.

I ran hands-on training sessions for the Millennium Forum team and wrote detailed documentation so they could manage the system on their own. I stayed on for ongoing support when things came up.

HubSpot's subscription tier didn't cover the reporting depth the team needed, so I built a custom spreadsheet to track open rates, engagement, bounces, readability, and list performance against historical data. It became their go-to for meetings and annual reporting.

Results
400+ members enrolled and processed through automated workflows
65% email open rate across member communications
9 platforms integrated into a unified stack with HubSpot as the hub
Full automation of registration, onboarding, and member communications
Subscription commerce built from scratch on Shopify + ReCharge
Staff independence — team trained and managing day-to-day operations
Custom reporting that surfaced insights the CRM tier couldn't provide
Scalable foundation — system designed to grow with membership goals

Challenge

HubSpot was connected to Salesforce but wasn't really being used as a marketing platform. Salesforce handled recruitment and donors. HubSpot was more of a loose email tool, no structure for real campaign execution or clean segmentation.

Four recurring newsletters plus one-off sends for webinars, announcements, and outreach. 82,000 contacts. Most of the work was manual: rebuilding lists, recreating assets, double-checking fields before every send. Reporting was whatever HubSpot's subscription tier could show.

Approach

I designed and built four audience-specific monthly newsletter templates in custom HTML/CSS. Each one was tailored to its audience but built on a shared structure and branding system so the team could move faster without starting from zero every month. Recurring email production dropped from 3 to 5 hours per send to about 60 to 90 minutes.

I standardized the full campaign build process: audience targeting, suppression logic, testing, QA, and post-send review. Tightened how lists were built, formalized pre-send checks, and created a consistent workflow for recurring and one-off sends. Less friction, fewer last-minute fixes, and the whole thing stopped depending on one person remembering how it worked.

I built HubSpot workflows for data cleanup, field standardization, and record hygiene. Reorganized property structure and audience logic so lists were easier to trust. Within the first year, pre-send list troubleshooting dropped by about 65%.

HubSpot's reporting tier didn't go deep enough, so I built a custom spreadsheet model to track open rates, click rates, engagement trends, bounces, unsubscribes, and historical comparisons across audiences. Reporting prep went from hours of manual compilation to a repeatable process that took under 45 minutes. It became the system for team reviews and year-end discussions.

I wrote 50+ SOPs covering campaign processes, template usage, segmentation rules, QA expectations, and performance tracking. The team got a stable operating model that didn't depend on tribal knowledge.

Results
70%+ faster email builds, recurring production dropped from 3 to 5 hours to 60 to 90 minutes
30%+ higher engagement on segmented sends vs. non-segmented
35–45% open rates and 2–12% CTR across newsletter audiences
65% less list troubleshooting through automated data hygiene workflows
82K contacts maintained with cleaner segmentation and field consistency
Reporting prep under 45 min with custom performance tracking model
50+ SOPs documenting end-to-end campaign and platform operations
Standardized campaign execution across targeting, QA, testing, and post-send review
