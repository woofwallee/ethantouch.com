About

Ethan Touch

I've spent seven years inside HubSpot — building workflows, connecting platforms, and fixing the things nobody notices until they break. I like it because it's one of the few CRMs that doesn't fight you. It's easy to learn, easy to teach, and the people behind it actually care about the community. When I'm home with family, we cook, watch movies, and throw karaoke parties that I'm told are "a lot" but never get complaints.

Challenge

Upstream USA works with hospitals and clinics on contraceptive care. They pulled leads from multiple healthcare conferences a year, plus webinars, paid campaigns, form fills, and content downloads. High volume from multiple channels, all year round.

No system to sort any of it. Someone sat with a spreadsheet and cross-referenced every contact: new lead, existing partner, or disqualified. That took hours. Sometimes days. By the time anyone followed up, the lead had gone cold.

HubSpot was live but not connected to Salesforce, which the recruitment team used every day.

Approach

Five routing workflows, one per channel: conferences, webinars, paid media, forms, and content downloads. Same logic, different entry points. Each one tagged, sorted, and routed leads into channel-specific drip campaigns. Custom properties mapped to Salesforce kept data clean across both platforms.

Every contact got classified automatically. MQL or SQL. Lifecycle stage, source, and lead score assigned on entry. Custom properties captured the first interaction so the team had context before reaching out.

Duplicate detection was built in. If a lead came from a company Upstream already worked with, the workflow caught it, updated the record, and tagged it as existing. No overlapping messages.

MQLs and SQLs synced to Salesforce in real time. SQLs entered drip campaigns with Calendly links for scheduling. Bookings triggered notifications and logged to the contact record. Before this, follow-up meant forwarding emails and hoping.

Email templates designed in Beefree and deployed through HubSpot. Subject lines, targeting, suppression, A/B testing, post-send reporting. Each department's drip campaign was built and handed off to a trained owner with a clear path back for updates.

Lead list imports got a process too. Data requirements defined upfront, shared inbox for visibility, and a clear handoff from collection to formatting to import.

HubSpot's reporting tier wasn't deep enough, so I built a custom tracking model benchmarked against historical data. It became the team's go-to for meetings and year-end reviews.

Results
Nearly all manual sorting eliminated, from hours or days to near-instant
Same-day lead response where there used to be multi-day delays
Dozens of workflows built across lead routing, drip campaigns, data hygiene, and operations
Full pipeline visibility across MQL/SQL stages for all teams
Automated scheduling replaced manual email forwarding via Calendly integration
Stronger open rates through timely, channel-specific messaging
Better segmentation through automated tagging by source, lifecycle, and company
Cross-team alignment with all activity visible across marketing, recruitment, and ops

Challenge

Nine platforms connected into one stack: Squarespace, Hivebrite, Circles, Zoom, Google Sheets, Gmail, Shopify, ReCharge Payments, and SurveyMonkey. All linked through native integrations and Zapier.

Member data migrated from legacy systems and spreadsheets into HubSpot. Contact model restructured with custom properties built for the program's specific needs.

Led the migration to Shopify with ReCharge Payments. Built a subscription-based membership system that synced back to HubSpot through plugins and Zapier.

Three core workflows: a registration and welcome series for new members, automated meeting reminders with personalized time slots and group numbers, and lifecycle communications that walked members through the full program.

Full email system designed around templates, branding, and automation sequences. The right message at the right time. No manual sends required.

Hands-on training for the Millennium Forum team with detailed documentation. They managed the system independently after launch. I stayed on for support when needed.

HubSpot's reporting tier wasn't deep enough, so I built a custom spreadsheet: open rates, engagement, bounces, readability, and list performance, all benchmarked historically. It became the team's go-to for meetings and annual reviews.

Challenge

HubSpot was connected to Salesforce but wasn't being used as a marketing platform. Salesforce handled recruitment and donors. HubSpot was a loose email tool with no structure for campaign execution or clean segmentation.

Multiple recurring newsletters plus one-off sends for webinars, announcements, and outreach across a large contact database. Approach

Newsletter templates built in custom HTML/CSS. Each one tailored to its audience but built on shared structure and branding. The team stopped rebuilding from scratch every month. Production time dropped significantly — what used to take most of a day now took about an hour.

Campaign process standardized across targeting, suppression, testing, QA, and post-send review. Pre-send checks formalized. Recurring and one-off sends followed the same workflow. No more depending on one person's memory.

Data hygiene workflows built in HubSpot for cleanup, field standardization, and record hygiene. Property structure reorganized and audience logic rebuilt. Lists became trustworthy. Pre-send troubleshooting dropped significantly in the first year.

HubSpot's reporting wasn't deep enough, so I built a custom model: open rates, click rates, engagement trends, bounces, unsubscribes, and historical comparisons. Reporting prep went from hours to under 45 minutes. It became the system for team reviews and year-end discussions.

50+ SOPs covering campaign processes, template usage, segmentation rules, QA expectations, and performance tracking. The team got a stable operating model. No tribal knowledge required.

Email Performance Analysis

A national nonprofit had a year of email data across three audience segments with no way to know what was working. I pulled the metrics, benchmarked them against industry averages, and built an interactive dashboard that turned raw numbers into clear priorities.

Challenge

The organization sent emails to three distinct audiences: SQLs, Customers, and MQLs. Each segment had different engagement patterns, but nobody had compared them side by side or against external benchmarks. Decisions came down to gut feel.

Open rates were climbing, but read rates were collapsing. Glance rates spiked dramatically across every segment. People opened emails but didn't read them. Without data to prove it, the team kept writing long-form content that nobody finished.

There was no frame of reference. Is that open rate low or competitive? Is that click rate decent or underperforming? The team had no way to know where they stood against other nonprofits their size.

Approach

I pulled a full year of email performance data across all three segments: open rates, click rates, click-to-open rates, bounce rates, unsubscribe rates, read rates, and glance rates. Every metric broken out by segment and compared year over year.

Benchmarked everything against national nonprofit averages from four industry sources: Neon One, MailerLite, M+R Benchmarks, and Nonprofit Tech for Good. Each metric rated as above, at, or below benchmark with context on what the gap meant.

Built an interactive dashboard with Chart.js. Segment filters update every chart and KPI card in real time. Benchmark bands overlay the bar charts so you can see where you stand at a glance. Single HTML file, no server needed.

Translated the data gaps into six prioritized recommendations. High priority: clean the customer list (bounce rate well above ceiling), shorten email copy (read rates declining), and fix subject lines for the weakest segment. Medium priority: optimize CTAs, audit send cadence, and replicate the content patterns behind the best engagement.

Results
Full benchmark scorecard across 5 core metrics for all 3 segments, rated against national averages
Interactive dashboard with segment filtering, YoY comparison charts, and benchmark overlay bands
Reading behavior shift identified: read rates declining, glance rates up dramatically across all segments
Top segment CTOR at 1.5x benchmark, validating content strategy for that audience
6 prioritized recommendations with specific actions tied to benchmark gaps
List hygiene issue surfaced: bounce rate well above ceiling in one segment, flagged before it damaged deliverability
