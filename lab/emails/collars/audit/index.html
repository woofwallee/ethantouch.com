Auditing the Memorial Weekend send from Collars Dog Resort.

Collars Dog Resort is a two-location Kansas City dog boarding, daycare, training, and grooming business with houses in Olathe and Waldo. On May 6 they sent two near-identical Memorial Weekend emails, one per location, both built on the same Gingr template. This is a short, specific look at what's working and what's broken across both sends, paired with a reimagined version of the Olathe email at the bottom.

Findings

Seven things, in order of impact.

01
Footer links — broken on Waldo, sloppy on Olathe
High

I followed every footer link in both sends. Same Gingr template, two completely different execution states.

Waldo — Dayplay, Board, Groom, Testimonials, About Us all 404 on the apex domain. Directions points to a Google Search, not a map. Instagram icon links to @collarclubkc, which Instagram returns "page not available" for.

Olathe — service subdomain links work, but About Us drops customers on a puppy-training page, Testimonials goes to Google Search, and the Instagram icon links to @collarclubolathe, also deleted.

Five of seven Waldo links dead. About Us misrouted on Olathe. Both Instagram icons dead. Neither send has a real testimonials destination.

The fix

Repoint Waldo to the waldo.collarsdogresort.com/{slug}/ pattern Olathe already uses. Repoint Olathe's About Us. Build a testimonials page on each subdomain or strip the link. The reimagined Olathe send drops the service strip entirely and keeps only working social icons.

02
Pitch vote campaign broken on both sends, different bugs each
High

Same Best of KC promo. Both sends broke it differently:

  • Olathe "Cast Your Vote!" button → Gingr account login screen. Customers can't vote at all from this link.
  • Waldo "Cast Your Vote!" text link works, but the Best of KC badge image next to it links to thepitchkc.com/bestofkc20/ — the 2020 archive, not the 2026 round.

The Pitch round ran April 22 through May 13, 2026. The send already went out. Lost clicks aren't recoverable through a template fix.

The fix

1. Optional recovery send if voting is still meaningfully open when this is read. Two paragraphs to Olathe subscribers, working URL as the only CTA. Owning the bug in the open earns more trust than hoping nobody noticed.

2. Pre-send link check on every campaign. Two-minute QA that follows every CTA to its real destination before send. One dead CTA shouldn't take down a multi-nomination vote drive. The reimagined Olathe send wires all four nominations to their real Pitch URLs by default — and each link deep-links straight to the category Collars is in, so customers don't have to scroll Pitch's full ballot of 200+ nominations across multiple pages to find the right four. One tap, one vote, done.

03
Social handles drift across locations
Medium

Three naming systems live at once: legal entity The Collar Club, LLC, consumer brand Collars Dog Resort, and the legacy "Collar Club KC" handles still wired into the footer:

LinkedInmissing from footercollarsdogresort (new)

Both Instagram icons point to deleted accounts. Every IG click is a credibility hit. New customers searching for "Collars Dog Resort" on social won't find the right account.

The fix

Pick one pattern and migrate. @collarsdogresort as the master handle, with location-specific accounts if useful. Add LinkedIn to the Olathe footer.

04
Subject and preheader leave open rate on the table
Medium

Subject works (emoji is good). Preheader was never set — both sends auto-pull the H1 (Memorial Weekend Boarding!) as preview text, repeating the subject line.

Industry Benchmark Litmus State of Email 2026 · Jay Schwedelson, p. 30
"If one variant has an emoji and one doesn't, and the emoji one gets a 20% higher open rate, the emoji one wins."

Collars keeps the emoji. The preheader is wasted — the second-most-read line of copy after the subject.

The fix

Set a real preheader on every send. For Memorial Weekend: Holiday weekends fill quickly. Book before they're gone. Zero send-time cost, measurable open-rate lift.

05
Lands in Promotions, with no signals to climb out
Medium

Gmail sorted this into Promotions, where it competes with Target and Best Buy for attention instead of landing in Primary.

Industry Trend Litmus State of Email 2026 · p. 6
"Senders that act like a single human writing to a single subscriber climb out of Promotions; senders that look templated stay there."

The templated footer and broadcast tone are exactly what Inbox AI uses to keep email parked in Promotions.

The fix

Personalize the From line ("Alan from Collars" instead of "Collars Dog Resort"). Use the dog's name in the body when available. Reduce image-to-text ratio. The reimagined send leaves room for a [Pet Name] token in the CTA — a 30-second Gingr change.

06
Two-location segmentation half-implemented
Low

Two separate Memorial Weekend sends went out same day, one per location. Right move. But neither cross-references the other ("Waldo full? Olathe still has spots"), and the body copy is near-identical apart from the address. Customers who overflow between locations get no signal that the flexibility exists.

The fix

One-line footer cross-reference on each send: "Hosting at our [other-location] house too. Book there if your home location fills."

07
The multi-section newsletter existed — then stopped
Medium

Through 2025, Collars sent multi-section newsletters mixing promo with relationship segments. A few from the inbox:

  • Sep 19, 2025 — "Training Classes Next Week | Dog of the Month | Staff Spotlight: Lily"
  • Aug 14, 2025 — "Training Class | Hope 4 La Paws Rescue | Labor Day Boarding"
  • Jun 11, 2025 — "New Grooming Updates | A Few Life Lessons from Your Dog"
  • May 13, 2025 — "Petiquette | Construction Update | Dog of the Month"

Dog of the Month, Staff Spotlights, community partnerships, behind-the-scenes — all in rotation. Then in early 2026 the format collapsed. The twelve sends from January through May 2026 are all single-topic promos. Every email asks for money or a sign-up. The relationship segments stopped.

Industry Insight Litmus State of Email 2026 · Ann Handley, p. 28
"A newsletter, in particular, is less about the news, and more about the letter."

Newsletters are 90% more likely to be the top-performing email type at pure-B2C brands. Collars used to know this. The format proved out for a year.

The fix

Bring it back. Pick any three of Dog of the Month, Staff Spotlight, community story, behind-the-scenes — combine with the month's promo. The pattern already worked. The customers who opened the September 2025 send are still on the list.

Why the reimagine runs longer (and why that's the point)

The reimagined email is taller because it does more. The original is one block of text, one CTA, and a broken vote line. The reimagine differentiates the two suite types with pricing, runs a transparency block with real Olathe photos, deep-links the four Pitch nominations directly to their categories so customers skip the scroll-hunt through Pitch's full ballot, and lands the holiday CTA inside a save-the-dates band. The format shift, from promo to letter, is the move Litmus is calling out as the highest-ROI change in B2C email right now.

Industry Insight Litmus State of Email 2026 · Ann Handley, p. 28
"From broadcasting to corresponding. A newsletter, in particular, is less about the news, and more about the letter."

Letters take more space than broadcasts. The extra height is the brand showing up like a person, not a Gingr template.

Revenue Data Litmus State of Email 2026 · p. 50
"Newsletters are 90% more likely to be the most engaging or generate the most ROI in pure B2C companies compared to B2B/B2C hybrids."

B2C newsletter priority is up 12% from 2025. Pure B2C brands like Collars are the segment where the longer, letter-style format converts hardest.

The length isn't filler. Each section earns its scroll: suite pricing answers the most common pre-booking question, transparency photos turn "no crates" from a tagline into proof, the four deep-linked vote buttons recover the broken CTA from the original AND skip the scroll-hunt — Pitch's ballot has hundreds of nominations spread across multiple categories, and one tap on each button lands the customer on the exact Collars page without browsing — and the save-the-dates band gives the holiday push its own real estate instead of being a single line at the top. Cut any of it and the email drops back toward the broadcast format Inbox AI is already deprioritizing.
