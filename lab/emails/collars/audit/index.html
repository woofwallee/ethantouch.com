Findings

What I caught in three minutes.

01
Footer link integrity — broken on Waldo, sloppy on Olathe
High

Same Gingr template, two completely different execution states. I followed every footer link in both sends to its real destination. Waldo's footer is mostly broken; Olathe's is mostly correct but has lazy substitutions.

Waldo (5 of 7 service-strip links return 404, plus a dead Instagram link):

  • Dayplay → http://collarsdogresort.com/dayplay/ — 404
  • Board → http://collarsdogresort.com/boarding/ — 404
  • Groom → http://collarsdogresort.com/grooming/ — 404
  • Testimonials → http://collarsdogresort.com/reviews/ — 404
  • About Us → http://collarsdogresort.com/about-us/ — 404
  • Directions → Google Search results for "collar club" (not a real map link)
  • Contact Us → tel:8168328285 (mislabeled as a page link, actually a phone tap)
  • Instagram icon → instagram.com/collarclubkc/ — account is gone ("Sorry, this page isn't available")

Olathe (4 issues):

  • Daycare → olathe.collarsdogresort.com/dog-day-care-in-olathe/ — works ✓
  • Boarding → olathe.collarsdogresort.com/dog-boarding-in-olathe/ — works ✓
  • Training → olathe.collarsdogresort.com/dog-training-in-olathe/ — works ✓
  • Testimonials → Google Search results page (no real testimonials page exists)
  • About Us → olathe.collarsdogresort.com/puppy-training-in-olathe/ — wrong destination (lands on a training page, not About)
  • Directions → Google Maps directions to 12732 S Pflumm Rd — works ✓
  • Instagram icon → instagram.com/collarclubolathe/ — account is gone ("Sorry, this page isn't available")

Same brand, same Gingr template, totally inconsistent execution. Waldo subscribers hit a dead end on five of seven links. Olathe subscribers click "About Us" and learn about puppy training. Neither send has a real testimonials destination.

The fix

Repoint the Waldo footer to the waldo.collarsdogresort.com/{slug}/ subdomain pattern that Olathe already uses correctly. Build a real testimonials page on each subdomain or strip the link. Repoint Olathe's About Us to a real about page. Or skip the whole service strip — the reimagined Olathe email drops it and keeps only social icons.

02
Pitch vote campaign is broken on both emails — different bugs each
High

Both sends ran the same Pitch Best of KC vote-for-us promo, and both sends broke it differently:

  • Olathe "Cast Your Vote!" button → collarclubolathe.portal.gingrapp.com/#/public/login. Recipients land on a Gingr account login screen, not the vote page. They can't vote at all from this link.
  • Waldo "Cast Your Vote!" text link → vote.thepitchkc.com ✓ (works). But the Best of KC badge image next to it links to thepitchkc.com/bestofkc20/ — that's the 2020 archive page, not the 2026 nomination round.

Net effect: every Olathe customer who clicked the vote button hit a dead end. Every Waldo customer who clicked the badge instead of the text link landed on a six-year-old archived contest. The vote campaign reached zero of the customers it was supposed to mobilize through these specific paths. The send already went out, so the lost clicks aren't recoverable through a template change. The Pitch Best of KC voting round ran April 22 through May 13, 2026. Whether a recovery send is worth running depends on how much voting time remained when this audit was read. The bigger value is in the process lesson, not the recapture.

The fix

1. Optional recovery send — only if voting is still meaningfully open. Short follow-up to Olathe subscribers: "Our vote link last week pointed to the wrong page. Here's the working one — vote.thepitchkc.com." Two paragraphs, the corrected URL as the only CTA, no other pitch. Worth running if there are still several days left in the voting window when this is read; not worth the operational lift if the round is closing within 48 hours or has already closed. Either way, the value is in owning the bug publicly more than in vote recapture — owners who fix mistakes in the open earn more trust than ones who hope nobody noticed. Same playbook applies the next time a campaign-bound CTA goes out broken.

2. Pre-send link check on every campaign going forward. This is the durable fix and the part that matters most. A two-minute QA pass that follows every CTA to its real destination before any campaign hits send. A single dead CTA should not be able to take down a multi-nomination vote drive again. The reimagined Olathe email already wires all four nominations directly to their real Pitch URLs by default.

03
Social handles drift across locations — three brand naming systems live at once
Medium

The legal entity is The Collar Club, LLC. The consumer-facing brand is Collars Dog Resort. The actual social handles linked from each email tell a third story — handles are mid-migration, partially per-location, and not consistent across channels:

Both Instagram links — Olathe's collarclubolathe and Waldo's collarclubkc — point to deleted accounts. Anyone clicking the IG icon on either email gets Instagram's "Sorry, this page isn't available" page. The brand isn't on Instagram at the linked handles at all right now, which means every social-icon click on IG is a credibility hit. Three of five social handles still carry the legacy "Collar Club" naming on top of that. New customers searching for "Collars Dog Resort" on Instagram or Facebook won't land on the right account. Olathe customers don't see a LinkedIn link at all, even though one exists.

The fix

Pick one handle pattern and migrate everything. @collarsdogresort as the master handle, with optional @collarsdogresortolathe / @collarsdogresortwaldo if you want location-specific accounts. Add LinkedIn to the Olathe footer template too — it's a missed credibility signal.

04
Subject line and preheader leave revenue on the table
Medium

Olathe subject: Memorial Weekend Boarding at Collars Olathe 🐾. Waldo subject: same template with the location swapped. Functional but flat for both. Preheader: Memorial Weekend Boarding! (literally the first H1 of the email body, repeated as preview text — the preheader was never explicitly set on either send).

Industry Benchmark Litmus State of Email 2026 · Jay Schwedelson, p. 30
"Open rates are extremely important. Directionally, if one variant has an emoji and one doesn't, and the emoji one gets a 20% higher open rate, the emoji one wins."

Collars keeps the emoji ✓, but wastes the preheader — the second-most-read piece of copy after the subject.

The fix

Set a real preheader for every send. For Memorial Weekend, something like Holiday weekends fill quickly — book before they're gone. Adds zero send-time effort, lifts open rate measurably.

05
Lands in Gmail's Promotions tab, no engagement signals to climb out
Medium

Gmail's inbox sorting put this email in the Promotions tab, alongside every retailer pushing a holiday sale. That's the right tab for a promotional email, but it means Collars is competing with Target and Best Buy for attention rather than landing in Primary where personal correspondence sits.

Industry Trend Litmus State of Email 2026 · p. 6
"Inbox AI matured significantly in 2025 — Gmail 'most-relevant' Promotions sort, Apple Intelligence summaries, Yahoo Catch Up. Senders that act like a single human writing to a single subscriber climb out of Promotions; senders that look templated stay there."

Collars' templated multi-section footer and broadcast tone are exactly the signals Inbox AI uses to keep an email parked in Promotions.

The fix

Cut the broadcast tone. Personalize from line ("Alan from Collars" instead of "Collars Dog Resort"). Use the dog's name in the body when possible. Reduce image-to-text ratio. The reimagined email leaves room for a [Pet Name] token in the CTA, which is a 30-second template change in Gingr.

06
Two-location segmentation half-implemented
Low

Collars sent two separate Memorial Weekend emails the same day — one for Olathe, one for Waldo. That's the right move. But neither email cross-references the other location ("Waldo full? Olathe still has spots"), and the body copy is nearly identical between them apart from the address. Customers whose dog stays at one location and overflows to the other (a real pattern) get no signal that flexibility exists.

The fix

Add a one-line cross-reference in the footer of each location's email: "Hosting at our [other-location] house too — book there if your home location fills." The reimagined email's footer does this with a two-card location block (currently disabled per your direction, but ready to switch back on for campaigns where it helps).

07
The multi-section newsletter cadence existed — and then it stopped
Medium

Looking back across the last 12 months of Collars sends, the pattern shifted noticeably. Through 2025, Collars sent multi-section newsletters that mixed promotional content with real relationship segments. A few from the inbox:

  • Sep 19, 2025 — "Training Classes Next Week | Dog of the Month | Staff Member Spotlight: Lily"
  • Aug 14, 2025 — "Training Class | Hope 4 La Paws Rescue | Labor Day Boarding"
  • Jul 21, 2025 — "Collars Dog Resort: Change in Leadership"
  • Jun 20, 2025 — "Collars Building Signage Update"
  • Jun 11, 2025 — "New Grooming Updates | A Few Life Lessons from Your Dog"
  • May 13, 2025 — "Petiquette | Construction Update | Dog of the Month"
  • Jan 19, 2026 — "Welcome to our New GM, Liberty"

Dog of the Month, Staff Spotlights, community partnerships (Hope 4 La Paws), behind-the-scenes (signage, construction, leadership change), and storytelling pieces ("Life Lessons from Your Dog") were all in the rotation. Then sometime in early 2026, the format collapsed. The twelve sends from January through May 2026 are all single-topic promo emails — training class, daycare deal, grooming offer, Memorial Weekend boarding, vote-for-us, repeat. Every email asks for money or a sign-up. The relationship segments stopped.

Industry Insight Litmus State of Email 2026 · Ann Handley, p. 28
"From broadcasting to corresponding. A newsletter, in particular, is less about the news, and more about the letter."

Newsletters are 90% more likely to be the top-performing email type at pure-B2C brands. Collars used to know this — the multi-section format proved out for a year.

The fix

Bring back the multi-section format. One Dog of the Month, one Staff Spotlight, one community/partner story, one behind-the-scenes update — pick any three and combine with the month's promo. The pattern already worked for Collars; it doesn't need to be reinvented. The customers who opened the September 2025 send are still on the list.
