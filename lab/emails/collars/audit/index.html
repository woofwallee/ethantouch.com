A quick audit of the Memorial Weekend send.

A short, specific look at what's working and what's broken in the most recent Collars Dog Resort marketing email. Six findings, one location-segmentation gap, and a list of fixes that take less than a week to implement. Paired with a reimagined version of the same email.

Source: "Memorial Weekend Boarding at Collars Waldo 🐾" Sender: contact@collarsdogresort.com via marketing.gingrapp.com Sent: May 6, 2026 · 12:26 PM Inbox tab: Promotions

Seven issues, none of them complicated.

Findings

What I caught in three minutes.

01
Six dead links in the footer service strip
High

The footer of the Memorial Weekend email links to Dayplay, Board, Groom, Testimonials, About Us, Directions, and Contact Us. Five of them point to collarsdogresort.com/{slug}/ URLs that don't resolve, and the Instagram handle (collarclubkc) is orphaned.

  • collarsdogresort.com/about-us/ — broken
  • collarsdogresort.com/reviews/ — broken
  • collarsdogresort.com/grooming/ — broken
  • collarsdogresort.com/boarding/ — broken
  • collarsdogresort.com/dayplay/ — broken
  • instagram.com/collarclubkc/ — orphaned (account no longer maintained at this handle)

Every send goes out with the same bad links. A subscriber who clicks the wrong link doesn't blame Gingr; they blame Collars.

The fix

Either point the links to the correct subdomain URLs (olathe.collarsdogresort.com/dog-boarding-in-olathe/ works; the apex domain doesn't), or strip the service link strip out of the footer template entirely. The reimagined version drops it and keeps only social icons.

02
"Cast Your Vote" CTA in the Olathe email points to the wrong page
High

Both Memorial Weekend emails (Waldo and Olathe) include a Cast Your Vote! button promoting the Pitch Best of KC nomination. The Waldo version links to the actual Pitch voting page, where customers can vote. The Olathe version links to the Collars Gingr member portal — recipients land on a logged-in account page that has nothing to do with voting. The campaign is unrunnable for half the customer base.

This is the highest-impact issue in the audit. Every Olathe customer who clicked "Cast Your Vote" got an immediate dead-end and almost certainly did not vote. The Pitch nomination round closes; that conversion window is already gone.

The fix

Repoint the Olathe template's vote CTA to vote.thepitchkc.com (specifically the boarding/daycare nomination page). 30 seconds in Gingr. After that, set up a pre-send link checker on every campaign so a single broken CTA doesn't take down a full vote drive again. The reimagined version wires all four nominations to their actual Pitch URLs by default.

03
Brand handles still using the old "Collar Club KC" name
Medium

The legal entity is The Collar Club, LLC. The consumer-facing brand is Collars Dog Resort. But the social handles still read:

  • Facebook: facebook.com/CollarClubKC/
  • Instagram: instagram.com/collarclubkc/

LinkedIn already migrated to collarsdogresort. Facebook and Instagram are on the old name. New customers searching for "Collars Dog Resort" on social won't find the right account on the first try.

The fix

Migrate Facebook and Instagram handles to collarsdogresort (or a near variant if taken) and add 301-style profile redirect notices on the old accounts for 90 days.

04
Subject line and preheader leave revenue on the table
Medium

Subject: Memorial Weekend Boarding at Collars Waldo 🐾. Functional but flat. Preheader: Boarding Memorial Weekend! (literally the first H1 of the email body, repeated as preview text — the preheader was never set).

Per Litmus 2026 (Jay Schwedelson): "Open rates are extremely important. Directionally, if one variant has an emoji and one doesn't, and the emoji one gets a 20% higher open rate, the emoji one wins." The email keeps the emoji ✓ but wastes the preheader, the second-most-read piece of copy after the subject.

The fix

Set a real preheader for every send. For Memorial Weekend, something like Holiday weekends fill quickly — book before they're gone. Adds zero send-time effort, lifts open rate measurably.

05
Lands in Gmail's Promotions tab, no engagement signals to climb out
Medium

Gmail's inbox sorting put this email in the Promotions tab, alongside every retailer pushing a holiday sale. That's the right tab for a promotional email, but it means Collars is competing with Target and Best Buy for attention rather than landing in Primary where personal correspondence sits.

Per Litmus 2026 (p. 6), Inbox AI matured significantly in 2025 — Gmail "most-relevant" Promotions sort, Apple Intelligence summaries, Yahoo Catch Up. Senders that act like a single human writing to a single subscriber climb out of Promotions; senders that look templated stay there.

The fix

Cut the broadcast tone. Personalize from line ("Alan from Collars" instead of "Collars Dog Resort"). Use the dog's name in the body when possible. Reduce image-to-text ratio. The reimagined email leaves room for a [Pet Name] token in the CTA, which is a 30-second template change in Gingr.

06
Two-location segmentation half-implemented
Low

Collars sent two separate Memorial Weekend emails the same day — one for Olathe, one for Waldo. That's the right move. But neither email cross-references the other location ("Waldo full? Olathe still has spots"), and the body copy is nearly identical between them apart from the address. Customers whose dog stays at one location and overflows to the other (a real pattern) get no signal that flexibility exists.

The fix

Add a one-line cross-reference in the footer of each location's email: "Hosting at our [other-location] house too — book there if your home location fills." The reimagined email's footer does this with a two-card location block (currently disabled per your direction, but ready to switch back on for campaigns where it helps).

07
Sends only happen for holidays and promos — no relationship cadence
Medium

Your last six sends across April and May are all transactional or promotional: training class enrollment, summer grooming offer, half-off Tuesday daycare, Memorial Weekend boarding (×2), creative color grooming. Every email asks for money. None of them just write to subscribers.

Per Litmus 2026 (Ann Handley): "From broadcasting to corresponding. A newsletter, in particular, is less about the news, and more about the letter." Newsletters are 90% more likely to be the top-performing email type at pure-B2C brands. Collars is leaving the highest-engagement category unused.

The fix

Add a monthly "From the Collars Family" letter. A real photo from one of the houses, a single piece of dog-care advice, one staff or pup spotlight. Three paragraphs. No CTA. The relationship asset that sets up every promo email that follows it.

What I'd own in week one.

01

Patch the dead links

Fix or remove the six broken footer links across the Gingr template. One afternoon.

02

Migrate social handles

Move Facebook and Instagram off the old "CollarClubKC" name to match the LinkedIn migration. New customer search match on day one.

03

Set real preheaders for every send

Add the second-most-read line of copy back into every email. No platform changes, just discipline.

04

Build the [Pet Name] token

Wire Gingr customer data into Pet Name and Pet First Name merge fields. Personalize one CTA per send.

05

Cross-reference the locations

Add the "also at our other house" footer line. Improves conversion when a location fills up. Same email infrastructure.

06

Draft the first relationship letter

One newsletter, one staff or pup spotlight, no CTA. Schedule it for the first week of next month. Set the cadence.

The reimagined send.

Same campaign, rebuilt as live HTML. Brand-locked palette, real Olathe photos, the Best of KC vote module integrated, mobile and dark-mode tested.
